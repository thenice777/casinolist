## Competitive Analysis

### Online Casino Review Space (CROWDED - Don't compete directly)

| Site | Strength | Monthly Traffic | Gap |
|------|----------|-----------------|-----|
| AskGamblers | 18 years, 10K casinos, dispute resolution | ~3M | Ignores physical |
| Casino.guru | Player protection, complaints | ~1.6M | No physical, basic UX |
| Casino.org | Since 1995, Forbes featured | ~1M+ | Pure online focus |
| Gambling.com | Well-funded, broad | ~800K | Generic affiliate approach |

### Physical Casino Directories (WEAK - Opportunity here!)

| Site | Casinos Listed | Strength | Weakness |
|------|---------------|----------|----------|
| World Casino Directory | Extensive | Established | Dated design, no map |
| Near-Me Casino | 2,000+ | Geolocation | Basic UX, no ratings |
| PlaySafeTrust | 4,300 | Verification | No discovery experience |
| CasinoLineup | Unknown | Photos/reviews | Not comprehensive |

### Strategic Insight

**Don't try to out-AskGamblers AskGamblers.** They have 18 years head start on online casino reviews.

**Instead, own the physical casino experience** - it's wide open:
- No beautiful interactive map exists
- No premium Michelin-style positioning
- No sophisticated rating systems
- No cross-channel player understanding

**Then use physical casino authority to bridge to online:**
- "Trust us for physical casinos? Trust us for online too"
- Cross-promote: "Love Bellagio? Try their online partner..."
- Unique SEO: Destination + casino keywords no one targets

## Data Sourcing Strategy

### Physical Casinos - Scrape + Verify Approach

**Sources to Aggregate:**
1. Google Maps API - Location data, reviews, hours
2. TripAdvisor - Reviews, photos
3. World Casino Directory - Existing data
4. Wikipedia - Notable casino pages
5. Casino websites - Direct verification

**Process:**
1. Scrape basic data (name, location, website)
2. Enrich with Google Places data
3. Verify and deduplicate
4. Add CasinoList original ratings/content over time

**Priority Cities (Initial 500 casinos):**
- Las Vegas (~200)
- Macau (~50)
- Monaco (~5)
- London (~15)
- Atlantic City (~15)
- Singapore (~5)
- Australia (Melbourne, Sydney, etc.) (~30)
- Europe major cities (~100)
- Asia other (~50)
- Rest of world (~30)

### Online Casinos

**Sources:**
1. AskGamblers/Casino.guru public data (what's licensed where)
2. Casino license registries (MGA, UKGC, Curacao)
3. Affiliate program networks

**Priority:** Focus on top 100 most reputable casinos initially. Quality over quantity.

## Technical Architecture Notes

### Stack Decision
- **Next.js 14** - SEO, performance, React ecosystem
- **Neon PostgreSQL** - Serverless, scales, geographic queries (PostGIS)
- **Mapbox or MapLibre** - Interactive maps
- **Vercel** - Deployment, edge functions
- **Tailwind** - Rapid UI development

### Key Technical Considerations
1. **PostGIS** for geographic queries (casinos near location)
2. **ISR** (Incremental Static Regeneration) for casino pages
3. **Edge caching** for map tiles
4. **Structured data** (JSON-LD) for SEO
